HOW TO PLAY

THE GOAL

Control all 60 territories — alone, or together with your alliance. Each round is worth 60 war points, split equally among the winners (solo: 60 · pair: 30 + 30 · trio: 20 each). Points persist across rounds on the scoreboard.

YOUR TURN

Turn order is shuffled fresh every round (the little numbers on the roster show this round's order). On your turn you may Join an alliance (once), Defect (once), Capture territories (up to 3 attacks), and End Turn — diplomacy is legal at any moment of your turn, before or after fighting. One rule of honor: while your alliance offer is out, your armies hold — no attacking until it is answered or you rescind it.

COMBAT — THE ATTACKER LEAVES ONE BEHIND

A territory needs 2+ force to attack a neighbor. The attacker rolls value − 1 dice (one stays home to hold the flag); the defender rolls all of theirs. Higher total wins — and ties go to the defender.

CASUALTIES — THE DEFEATED LEAVE THE BOARD

Win or lose, your attacking territory drops to 1 (the garrison stays). Win: the defenders are wiped out and your force marches in — the captured territory holds your force − 1. Lose: your attacking force is wiped out where it stood; the defender doesn't lose a soul.

Every battle removes force from the board — the war only gets leaner. A failed attack costs you everything you marched with.

ALLIANCES — WIN TOGETHER, SPLIT THE SPOILS

One alliance per commander. Propose a pact to a free agent, or request to join an existing alliance — every member must approve. Allies cannot attack each other, and if your bloc holds all 60 territories, you all win and split the 60 points. Recipients answer from their own seat, even off-turn — but the window is your live turn: an unanswered offer expires when your turn ends, and while it is out your armies cannot attack. You may rescind a pending offer at any time, which refunds your join action and frees your armies the same turn.

BETRAYAL

Defect on your turn to abandon your alliance — your former allies are notified, and their borders open to you. One join and one defect per turn, at any moment of it — you may even strike, defect, and strike again. Time your treachery well.

ELIMINATION

Lose your last territory and you're out of the round — removed from your alliance, watching as a spectator. Your war points survive; the next round is a fresh start.
